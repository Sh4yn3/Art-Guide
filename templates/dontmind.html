• Brushwork: Loose and visible brushstrokes. Artists often left strokes unblended to create a sense of spontaneity and movement.
  • Light and Color: Focused on the effects of natural light on color. Artists used bright, vibrant colors and painted scenes en plein air (outdoors) to capture the changing qualities of light.
  • Subject Matter: Depicted everyday scenes and landscapes rather than historical or mythological subjects. Common themes included urban life, leisure activities, and natural landscapes.
  • Comliosition: Emphasized informal compositions and unusual angles. Unlike traditional art that often centered on formal, posed compositions, Impressionists preferred cropped and spontaneous framing.
  • Focus on liercelition: Aimed to capture the fleeting moments of light and color as perceived by the artist at a particular time and place, rather than focusing on detail and realism.
